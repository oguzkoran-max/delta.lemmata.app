# P017 Blind Critic Scorecards (verbatim, 2026-07-21)

Method: three independent reviewer agents, each given ONLY the two anonymous
contact sheets (labels A/B/C) and the scoring rubric — no implementation
explanation, no intended score, no author defence, no effort context. Run in
parallel; outputs below are unedited. These scores grade CONCEPT-STAGE heroes,
not the final page; the ≥90/100 acceptance rubric applies after the
post-selection iteration loop.

## Critic 1 — senior editorial art director

CANDIDATE A: impact=7.3 originality=7.0 signature=7.2 typography=6.8 transformation=7.6 mobile=6.4 overall=7.1 generic=no
objections: 1) The layout is the most default premium split there is (copy-left, prop-right); the concept is ownable but the composition is off-the-shelf, and the headline scale is timid — the type cedes the entire stage to one prop. 2) The mid-stack slabs go muddy: two or three strata read as unlit noise rather than legible typographic specimens, so the "core sample of a corpus" idea only half-lands at first glance. 3) On mobile the signature object is demoted to garnish below the CTA cluster; the top half of the screen is an interchangeable text stack, which is exactly where the art direction should be fighting.

CANDIDATE B: impact=7.6 originality=5.5 signature=5.4 typography=8.5 transformation=5.0 mobile=7.0 overall=6.2 generic=no
objections: 1) The scroll transformation is a broken promise: monumental type resolves into a minuscule, underdesigned MFW tick chart that looks like a placeholder — the cinema dies at the exact moment the product argument begins. 2) Giant staircase serif on a dark ground is the reigning trope of studio portfolios and type-foundry sites; strip the body copy and nothing on screen says stylometry — it fails the swap test toward foundries and agencies even if not toward SaaS. 3) Hierarchy in the second state is inverted: "One setting is not evidence." — the best line on the page — is set as a caption beneath re-entering display words, so decoration outranks argument.

CANDIDATE C: impact=5.0 originality=4.8 signature=4.4 typography=5.5 transformation=6.0 mobile=4.4 overall=5.0 generic=yes
objections: 1) The ghost manuscript is a low-contrast gray-green rectangle that rewards nothing at hero scale — ruled lines and three faint squiggles are not a signature visual, and the translucent-document prop is stock imagery for every contract/PDF tool. 2) The first viewport has no focal hierarchy: overline, headline, body, CTAs, and caption float in staggered alignments across the sheet, with cream type crossing the pale page area and losing contrast — this is the tasteful academic brochure the brief forbids. 3) Mobile spends most of the fold on a near-empty panel with the headline colliding with its bottom edge; the strongest beat (the documented/unknown rights ledger) is a spec table, not a cinematic payoff.

RANKING: A > B > C.
GREENLIGHT: Hybrid of A and B — keep A's stratigraphy object and its annotate-on-scroll transformation as the narrative spine, but import B's typographic nerve: display-scale headline setting (with the striped-infill word treatment on "text") so the first viewport is carried by type and prop together rather than prop alone. C is killed; its only salvageable idea, the documented/unknown metadata ledger, folds into A's annotation state.

## Critic 2 — motion design director

CANDIDATE A: impact=7.2 originality=7.6 signature=7.0 typography=6.8 transformation=8.2 mobile=6.4 overall=7.4 generic=no
objections: 1) Beneath the prop sits a stock SaaS armature — eyebrow / H1 / pill-CTA pair / copy-left-object-right split; the layout grid is off-the-shelf even if the artifact isn't. 2) In the scroll state the stack drifts right and crops out of frame top and bottom; if the exploded view can't be held composed in-frame, the choreography will read as parallax drift, not transformation. 3) The annotation payoff whispers — hairline leaders and tiny gray small-caps (GENRE…OCR) hugging the right edge, and RIGHTS · UNKNOWN, the narrative climax, is typographically indistinguishable from the other labels except for one thin orange tick.

CANDIDATE B: impact=7.6 originality=5.6 signature=5.4 typography=8.4 transformation=6.2 mobile=7.6 overall=6.7 generic=no
objections: 1) Full-bleed kinetic type is the most cloned move in award-site land (every foundry and studio portfolio does it); apart from the hatched "text" fill, nothing here is ownable to Delta or to stylometry. 2) State 2's lower half betrays the bar — the world the type was supposed to become is a spindly row of four MFW tick glyphs with caption-size labels, an underdesigned footnote posing as a diagram. 3) The pair promises headline-recompression plus new-section-arrival, which is exactly the disqualified pattern: once the four words settle, there is no persistent visual world left to transform for the rest of the page.

CANDIDATE C: impact=5.6 originality=6.0 signature=5.2 typography=6.0 transformation=5.4 mobile=6.3 overall=5.6 generic=no
objections: 1) The protagonist barely moves — between states the page keeps essentially the same pose and position while a side table of "documented" rows appears; that is a persistent background prop with content swapped beside it, not a transformation. 2) The prop itself is mush: low-contrast grey-green fill, faint rules, three orange scribbles, no internal typographic detail to reward proximity, so at hero scale it reads as a placeholder card. 3) The first viewport is compositionally broken — headline collides with the page's edge, the eyebrow sits half on the prop, and the lower-right quadrant is dead space; it looks unfinished rather than atmospheric.

RANKING: A > B > C.
GREENLIGHT: Hybrid A+B — A's stratigraphy stack is the only candidate whose two states genuinely promise one object exploding into labeled evidence layers, so it carries the scroll narrative. Graft B's typographic scale and hatch treatment onto A's headline beats, and kill A's SaaS split-grid so the stack owns the full frame from the first pixel.

## Critic 3 — brand identity strategist

CANDIDATE A: impact=7.5 originality=7.0 signature=7.0 typography=7.5 transformation=8.0 mobile=6.5 overall=7.3 generic=no
objections: 1) The stacked-planes object flirts hard with the dev-tool "layer stack" render cliché (Vercel/Linear lineage) — soft glow, vignette void, dark-mode product-render sheen; only the glyphs and punctuation inside the strata save it from being an infra-SaaS hero. 2) Emerald-pill CTA on near-black green is the default fashionable dark-SaaS color story; nothing in the palette is ownable, and the caption "conceptual visualisation · not an analysis result" openly confesses the signature image is illustration, not instrument. 3) Mobile crops the strata into a decorative texture strip — the core-sample metaphor (letters, punctuation, function words as sediment) is illegible exactly where half the audience meets it.

CANDIDATE B: impact=7.5 originality=5.5 signature=6.0 typography=8.5 transformation=6.5 mobile=7.0 overall=6.4 generic=no
objections: 1) Fails the logo-swap test worst of the three: oversized staggered Didone with one inline-striped word is the current awwwards editorial/type-foundry template — cover the logo and this is a foundry specimen page or a literary magazine, not a stylometry instrument. 2) There is no signature visual at all; philology + computation exists only in 12px body copy and one small MFW axis in the scroll state, so the entire identity hangs on a single striped word. 3) Template rhythm in the scroll state (re-staggered headline → paragraph → tick-mark row) — the "transformation" is a re-layout, not a metamorphosis; style outruns argument in the first viewport, where the product claim is a footnote to typographic theater.

CANDIDATE C: impact=6.0 originality=6.5 signature=5.5 typography=6.5 transformation=7.5 mobile=6.0 overall=6.2 generic=no
objections: 1) The translucent stacked pages are borderline glassmorphism — ghosted soft-edged planes reading as a stock "paper mockup" rather than a folio; the page carries no legible textual matter (faint rules and orange squiggles only), which is fatal for a direction whose whole claim is museum-quality documentality. 2) First-viewport hierarchy is unresolved: eyebrow, headline, paragraph, and CTA cluster mid-canvas on top of the object while the bottom-right of the viewport dies empty. 3) Mobile spends the top ~45% of the viewport on a low-contrast gray-green blur before a single word of meaning appears — atmosphere without information.

RANKING: A > B > C
GREENLIGHT: Greenlight A, hardened with B's typographic discipline: keep the text-strata core sample and its scroll reveal into provenance-labeled layers (GENRE/DATE/RIGHTS·UNKNOWN — the one image in the set that fuses computational feature-strata with philological apparatus, which no fintech, foundry, or AI startup could plausibly wear), but kill the glossy 3D glow in favor of a flat engraved-plate/instrument-drawing rendering and B's measurement-rule type system. C's annotated-ledger scroll idea should be folded into A's label rail; as a standalone direction its empty ghost-page can't survive the logo-swap against any "digital archive" template.
